## üéì Allocation Methodology Explained

### 1. Equal Weight
- Simple approach: divide capital equally among all strategies
- Pros: Simple, unbiased
- Cons: Doesn't account for risk or performance differences

### 2. Inverse Volatility
- Allocates more capital to less volatile strategies
- Pros: Risk-based, reduces portfolio volatility
- Cons: May underweight high-performing volatile strategies

### 3. Sharpe Weighted
- Allocates based on risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe ratio)
- Pros: Rewards efficient strategies
- Cons: May overweight strategies with limited capacity

### 4. Risk Parity
- Equalizes risk contribution from each strategy
- Pros: Balanced risk exposure
- Cons: Complex calculation, may not maximize returns

### 5. Max Sharpe
- Optimizes for maximum portfolio Sharpe ratio
- Pros: Best risk-adjusted returns
- Cons: May concentrate in few strategies
- **Recommended for this portfolio**
